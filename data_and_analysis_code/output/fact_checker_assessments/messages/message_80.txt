** Our explanation for veracity score **
Empirical studies indicate that significant increases in public sector wages can exert upward pressure on private sector wages. For instance, research on OECD countries found that a 1% rise in public sector wages leads to a 0.3% increase in private sector wages. This effect is attributed to the public sector's role as a benchmark in wage negotiations and its competition with the private sector for talent. In the UK context, studies have shown that while public sector wages adjust to private sector wages in the long run, there can be short-term spillovers where increases in public sector pay influence private sector wage growth. These spillovers are more pronounced in domestically oriented industries with lower worker bargaining power or those reliant on public sector inputs. Therefore, the claim that significant public sector wage increases can lead to broader upward pressure on wages in other sectors is largely accurate, though the magnitude and immediacy of the effect can vary based on industry characteristics and economic conditions.
