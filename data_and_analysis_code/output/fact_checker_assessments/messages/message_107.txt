"The San Francisco Public Library runs successful literacy programs."

** Our veracity score (0 = FALSE, 100 = TRUE) **
100/100

** Our explanation for veracity score **
The San Francisco Public Library (SFPL) operates several successful literacy programs. Project Read, established in 1983, has assisted over 7,000 adult learners in improving their reading and writing skills. In fiscal year 2022–2023 alone, volunteers contributed more than 1,300 hours to this program. ([sfpl.org](https://sfpl.org/releases/2023/12/04/san-francisco-public-librarys-project-read-celebrates-40-years-empowering-adult)) Additionally, SFPL's FOG Readers program provides structured, multisensory reading instruction to students in grades one through four, helping them overcome reading challenges. ([urbanlibraries.org](https://www.urbanlibraries.org/innovations/fog-readers-closing-the-reading-gap-at-sfpl)) The Bridge at Main offers a comprehensive curriculum, including technology training and adult literacy tutoring, serving over 5,000 patrons annually. ([urbanlibraries.org](https://www.urbanlibraries.org/innovations/start-here-the-bridge-at-main)) These initiatives demonstrate SFPL's commitment to enhancing literacy within the community.
