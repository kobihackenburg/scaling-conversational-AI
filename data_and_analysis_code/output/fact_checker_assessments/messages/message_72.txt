The 0.7% target is an internationally recognized commitment for developed countries to allocate 0.7% of their Gross National Income (GNI) to Official Development Assistance (ODA) aimed at supporting developing nations. This target was formalized by the United Nations General Assembly in 1970. The UK first achieved this target in 2013 and enshrined it into law with the International Development (Official Development Assistance Target) Act 2015. However, due to economic challenges, the UK temporarily reduced its ODA spending to 0.5% of GNI in 2021, with plans to return to the 0.7% target when fiscal conditions allow. [Source: House of Commons Library, 'The 0.7% aid target'](https://commonslibrary.parliament.uk/research-briefings/sn03714/)
