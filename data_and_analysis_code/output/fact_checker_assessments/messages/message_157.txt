The UK government is actively developing business models to provide certainty for companies investing in Carbon Capture, Usage, and Storage (CCUS). This includes implementing contracts for difference (CfDs) similar to those used for renewable energy. The 'Carbon Capture, Usage and Storage: a vision to establish a competitive market' document outlines the establishment of a UK CCUS market, aiming to unlock economic opportunities and address financial and regulatory barriers to deployment. Additionally, the 'Contracts for Difference (Electricity Supplier Obligations) (Amendment) Regulations 2024' enable payments to power CCUS plants under the dispatchable power agreement, a type of CfD designed to incentivize investment in power CCUS in the UK.
