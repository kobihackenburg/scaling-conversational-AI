Studies have shown that in poor countries, emigration tends to rise along with development, with countries that have grown from low income to upper middle income seeing their emigration rates almost triple on average. This trend is attributed to factors such as increased education levels, better access to information, and higher aspirations, all of which can inspire and facilitate migration. Therefore, the claim that improved economic conditions in a migrant's home country reduce the need for people to move abroad is generally inaccurate.
