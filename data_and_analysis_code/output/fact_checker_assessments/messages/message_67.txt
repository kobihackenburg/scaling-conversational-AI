** Our explanation for veracity score **
The claim is accurate. Security experts and military strategists have consistently recognized climate change as a 'threat multiplier.' For instance, the U.S. Department of Defense's 2014 Quadrennial Defense Review stated that climate change 'may exacerbate water scarcity and lead to sharp increases in food costs,' influencing resource competition and placing additional burdens on economies and governance institutions worldwide. Similarly, NATO has acknowledged climate change as the ultimate 'threat multiplier,' noting its potential to intensify extreme weather events and stress food and water supplies, thereby exacerbating political instability in vulnerable regions. These assessments underscore the consensus among security professionals that climate change can amplify existing vulnerabilities and conflicts. [Source: Institute For Governance & Sustainable Development](https://www.igsd.org/climate-change-a-growing-threat-multiplier-says-pentagon/), [Source: Bulletin of the Atomic Scientists](https://thebulletin.org/2017/05/nato-joins-the-pentagon-in-deeming-climate-change-a-threat-multiplier/)
