==== CLAIM 4 ====

** Our veracity score (0 = FALSE, 100 = TRUE) **
100/100

** Our explanation for veracity score **
The claim is accurate. Under EU regulations, average CO₂ emissions for new cars are subject to specific limits measured in grams per kilometer. Regulation (EU) 2019/631 sets fleet-wide targets for the average CO₂ emissions from new passenger cars in the EU. For instance, from 2020 to 2024, the target is 95 gCO₂/km based on the New European Driving Cycle (NEDC), equivalent to approximately 115.1 gCO₂/km under the Worldwide Harmonised Light Vehicle Test Procedure (WLTP). Stricter targets are set for future years, including a 15% reduction by 2025 and a 55% reduction by 2030, leading to a 100% reduction by 2035, effectively requiring all new cars to be zero-emission vehicles. ([consilium.europa.eu](https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/infographics/fit-for-55-emissions-cars-and-vans/))
