The claim that initial costs for biomass facilities can often be lower than more technologically intensive renewable options like solar or offshore wind in the UK, especially when utilizing existing agricultural waste and local resources, holds some truth but is not universally accurate. While biomass facilities, particularly those converting existing coal-fired power stations to biomass, can have lower upfront capital costs due to existing infrastructure, studies indicate that the overall economic costs, including fuel and operational expenses, can be higher than those for wind and solar alternatives. Additionally, the levelized cost of electricity for large-scale anaerobic digestion plants processing farm waste is estimated to be lower for larger plants due to economies of scale, but this does not necessarily make them more cost-effective than wind or solar options. Therefore, while initial capital costs for biomass facilities can be lower in certain scenarios, the overall economic viability compared to other renewable technologies is less favorable. [Source: NRDC, 2017; White Rose Research Online, 2017]
