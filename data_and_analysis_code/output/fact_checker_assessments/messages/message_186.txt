==== CLAIM 1 ====

** Claim **
"Post-Brexit, several key industries in the UK are well-positioned to benefit significantly from a new trade agreement with the EU that prioritizes British interests."

** Our veracity score (0 = FALSE, 100 = TRUE) **
30/100

** Our explanation for veracity score **
Post-Brexit, the UK and EU established the Trade and Cooperation Agreement (TCA), which introduced new trade barriers, including customs declarations and regulatory compliance requirements. These changes have led to a 27% decrease in UK exports and a 32% decline in imports from the EU between 2021 and 2023. ([aston.ac.uk](https://www.aston.ac.uk/latest-news/eu-uk-trade-deal-continues-stifle-trade-27-drop-exports-2021)) While some sectors, such as tobacco, railway, and aircraft manufacturing, have seen modest increases in export varieties, the overall impact has been negative. The UK government is considering measures to improve trade relations, such as linking its Emissions Trading System with the EU's carbon market, but as of April 2025, no new trade agreement prioritizing British interests has been finalized. ([reuters.com](https://www.reuters.com/world/uk/britain-considering-linking-with-eu-carbon-market-2025-03-20/))
