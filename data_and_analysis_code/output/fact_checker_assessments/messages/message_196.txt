"Many countries around the world are recognizing the importance of cybersecurity and are taking significant steps to enhance their digital defenses. Here's a look at some initiatives by other nations that illustrate the global trend towards prioritizing cyber defense:

1. **United States**: The U.S. has established comprehensive national cybersecurity strategies, including the creation of the Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) to protect critical infrastructure. The country invests heavily in cyber defense technologies and workforce development, implementing initiatives to secure federal systems and collaborate with private sectors.

After experiencing a large-scale cyber attack in 2007, the country developed a robust e-governance system with strong cybersecurity measures. Estonia also hosts the NATO Cooperative Cyber Defence Centre of Excellence.

It has established the National Cyber Directorate to oversee national defenses and collaborates closely with the private sector. Israel's innovative approach has led to a thriving cybersecurity industry that exports solutions worldwide.

The government has set up comprehensive public-private partnerships to bolster security measures across various sectors and supports research and development in cyber defense.

5. **Japan**: In preparation for the 2020 Tokyo Olympics, Japan significantly ramped up its cybersecurity efforts. The country developed a national strategy focusing on securing critical infrastructure, increasing cybersecurity awareness, and enhancing incident response capabilities.

6. **European Union**: The EU as a whole is working to strengthen cybersecurity through directives like the Network and Information Security (NIS) Directive, which sets security standards for critical infrastructure operators. Member states collaborate to share information and strengthen collective security measures.

These examples highlight a growing global movement towards prioritizing cyber defenses. By observing and implementing best practices from around the world, the U.K. can further enhance its capabilities, aligning with international standards and ensuring a secure digital future."

** Our explanation for veracity score **

==== CLAIM 10 ====

** Claim **
"Japan developed a national strategy focusing on securing critical infrastructure, increasing cybersecurity awareness, and enhancing incident response capabilities."

** Our veracity score (0 = FALSE, 100 = TRUE) **
100/100

** Our explanation for veracity score **
Japan has indeed developed a national strategy focusing on securing critical infrastructure, increasing cybersecurity awareness, and enhancing incident response capabilities. The Cybersecurity Strategy issued in September 2021 outlines objectives such as ensuring a free, fair, and secure cyberspace, improving socio-economic vitality, and building a society where people can live safe and secure lives. Measures include strengthening public-private coordination for information sharing and response, conducting comprehensive training and exercises to respond to sophisticated cyberattacks, and enhancing cooperation with like-minded countries. Additionally, the National Center of Incident Readiness and Strategy for Cybersecurity (NISC) coordinates cybersecurity policy by formulating strategies and policies for critical infrastructure protection. These efforts demonstrate Japan's commitment to bolstering its cybersecurity posture. Sources: [NISC](https://www.nisc.go.jp/eng/index.html), [Digital Watch Observatory](https://dig.watch/resource/japans-cybersecurity-strategy-2021).
