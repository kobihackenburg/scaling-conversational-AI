** Our explanation for veracity score **
Household coal burning significantly contributes to local air pollution, adversely affecting public health and causing environmental degradation. The World Health Organization reports that around 2.3 billion people worldwide cook using open fires or inefficient stoves fueled by coal and other solid fuels, generating harmful household air pollution responsible for an estimated 3.2 million deaths per year. Exposure to pollutants from coal combustion is linked to diseases such as stroke, ischaemic heart disease, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and lung cancer. Additionally, emissions from household coal burning contribute to ambient (outdoor) air pollution, further impacting environmental quality. Therefore, the claim is accurate. [Source: WHO, Household air pollution and health, 15 December 2023]
