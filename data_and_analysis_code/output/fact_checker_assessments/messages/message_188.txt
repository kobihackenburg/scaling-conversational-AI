Breeder reactors are a type of nuclear reactor designed to generate more fissile material than they consume. They achieve this by converting fertile isotopes, such as uranium-238 or thorium-232, into fissile isotopes like plutonium-239 or uranium-233 through neutron absorption and subsequent radioactive decay. This process allows breeder reactors to produce more fuel than they consume, effectively extending the nuclear fuel supply. ([britannica.com](https://www.britannica.com/technology/breeder-reactor))
