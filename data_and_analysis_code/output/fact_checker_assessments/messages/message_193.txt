==== CLAIM 2 ====

** Claim **
"Germany's environmental zones have contributed to reductions in particulate matter and nitrogen dioxide levels."

** Our veracity score (0 = FALSE, 100 = TRUE) **
85/100

** Our explanation for veracity score **
Studies indicate that Germany's environmental zones (Low Emission Zones) have effectively reduced particulate matter (PM10) concentrations. For instance, in Leipzig, the implementation of a Low Emission Zone led to a 60% decrease in black carbon and a 70% reduction in ultrafine particles at street sites. However, the impact on nitrogen dioxide (NO2) levels has been less significant, with some studies showing minimal reductions or even slight increases in NO2 concentrations. This discrepancy is partly due to modern diesel vehicles emitting less particulate matter but more NO2. Therefore, while environmental zones have substantially decreased particulate matter, their effect on nitrogen dioxide levels is limited. Sources: [Healthier Air due to the Low Emission Zone](https://www.tropos.de/en/current-issues/press-releases/details/healthier-air-due-to-the-low-emission-zone), [Low emission zones reduce PM10 mass concentrations and diesel soot in German cities](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24843918/), [Effectiveness of low emission zones: large scale analysis of changes in environmental NO2, NO and NOx concentrations in 17 German cities](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25115911/).
