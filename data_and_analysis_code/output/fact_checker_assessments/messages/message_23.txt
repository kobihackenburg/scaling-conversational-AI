** Our explanation for veracity score **
The claim is inaccurate. In the UK, Council Tax is not a fixed-rate system; it is based on property value bands. Properties are assigned to bands (A to H) based on their estimated market value as of 1 April 1991. Each band corresponds to a different tax rate, meaning that a smaller cottage would typically fall into a lower band and incur a lower tax than a larger, more expensive villa. Therefore, Sarah would not pay the same amount as her neighbors with larger properties. [Source: GOV.UK - How domestic properties are assessed for Council Tax bands](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/understand-how-council-tax-bands-are-assessed)
