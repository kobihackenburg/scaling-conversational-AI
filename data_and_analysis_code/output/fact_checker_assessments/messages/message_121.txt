** Our explanation for veracity score **
The claim is accurate. In England, families can receive up to 30 hours of free childcare per week for 3- and 4-year-olds during term time, provided parents meet certain employment criteria. Specifically, both parents (or the sole parent in a single-parent household) must be working and earning at least the equivalent of 16 hours per week at the National Minimum Wage or Living Wage, and neither parent should have an adjusted net income exceeding £100,000 per year. This entitlement is available for 38 weeks per year, aligning with school term times. ([gov.uk](https://www.gov.uk/check-eligible-free-childcare-if-youre-working))

** Our veracity score (0 = FALSE, 100 = TRUE) **
100/100

** Our explanation for veracity score **
The claim is accurate. In England, families with 2-year-olds who receive specific benefits are eligible for up to 15 hours of free childcare per week. Eligible benefits include Income Support, income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance, income-related Employment and Support Allowance, Universal Credit (with household income of £15,400 a year or less after tax), the guaranteed element of Pension Credit, and Child Tax Credit or Working Tax Credit (with household income of £16,190 a year or less before tax). Additionally, 2-year-olds may qualify if they are looked after by a local authority, have an Education, Health and Care (EHC) plan, receive Disability Living Allowance, or have left care under certain orders. This information is confirmed by the UK government's official website. ([gov.uk](https://www.gov.uk/help-with-childcare-costs/free-childcare-2-year-olds-claim-benefits))

This support is available for children up to 11 years old, and up to 16 years old if they are disabled. [Source: GOV.UK - Tax-Free Childcare](https://www.gov.uk/tax-free-childcare/eligibility)
