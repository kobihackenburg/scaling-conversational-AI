** Our explanation for veracity score **
The claim is accurate. In Canada, voters can prove their identity and address by showing one piece of government-issued ID with their photo, name, and current address, such as a driver's licence. Alternatively, they can present two pieces of ID, both showing their name and at least one with their current address. If a voter lacks these forms of ID, they can declare their identity and address in writing and have another registered voter from the same polling station vouch for them. The voucher must have acceptable identification and can vouch for only one person, except in long-term care institutions. These provisions are outlined by Elections Canada.
