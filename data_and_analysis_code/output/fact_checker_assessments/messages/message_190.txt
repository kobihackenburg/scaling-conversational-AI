==== CLAIM 7 ====

** Claim **
"Landowners whose properties are required for the railway can receive fair compensation above market value, facilitating a smooth transition."

** Our veracity score (0 = FALSE, 100 = TRUE) **
0/100

** Our explanation for veracity score **
In the UK, compensation for land acquired through compulsory purchase, such as for railway projects, is typically based on the open market value of the property, adhering to the principle of 'equivalence'—ensuring owners are neither better nor worse off financially due to the acquisition. This compensation does not generally exceed market value. Recent legislative changes, like the Levelling-up and Regeneration Act 2023, have introduced provisions allowing certain public authorities to exclude 'hope value'—the potential for future planning permission—from compensation assessments in specific cases, potentially reducing compensation below market value. Therefore, the claim that landowners receive compensation above market value is inaccurate. [Source: UK Government Guidance on Compulsory Purchase Compensation](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/compulsory-purchase-compensation-power-to-remove-hope-value)
