The claim that the U.K. could lead globally by implementing ethnic diversity quotas in corporate boardrooms is partially accurate. While the U.K. has made significant progress in increasing ethnic diversity on corporate boards through voluntary targets, it has not implemented mandatory quotas. The Parker Review set voluntary targets for FTSE 100 and FTSE 250 companies to have at least one director from an ethnic minority background by 2021 and 2024, respectively. By the end of 2022, 96% of FTSE 100 companies had met this target. However, these are voluntary targets, not legally mandated quotas. Therefore, while the U.K. is making strides in promoting boardroom diversity, it has not implemented ethnic diversity quotas.
