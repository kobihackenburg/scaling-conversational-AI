The National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC), established in 2016, has indeed mitigated thousands of cyber incidents, safeguarding both governmental and private sector interests in the UK. By October 2019, the NCSC had handled nearly 1,800 incidents since its inception, with a significant number originating from hostile nation-states. In the 12 months leading up to August 2019, the NCSC managed 658 incidents, supporting almost 900 victim organizations. These efforts have been instrumental in protecting the UK's critical infrastructure and private sector from cyber threats. [Source: NCSC Investigated 658 Serious Cybersecurity Incidents](https://www.govinfosecurity.com/ncsc-investigated-658-serious-cybersecurity-incidents-a-13286)

The U.K.'s National Cyber Strategy 2022 indeed outlines a comprehensive framework aimed at enhancing resilience, fostering collaboration with international allies, and driving innovation in cybersecurity technologies. The strategy is built around five core pillars: strengthening the UK cyber ecosystem, building a resilient and prosperous digital UK, taking the lead in technologies vital to cyber power, advancing UK global leadership and influence, and detecting, disrupting, and deterring adversaries. These pillars collectively aim to protect and promote the UK's interests in cyberspace. [Source: UK Government's National Cyber Strategy 2022]


==== CLAIM 4 ====

** Claim **
"Initiatives have been launched in the U.K. to protect critical infrastructure sectors like energy, healthcare, and finance from cyber threats."

** Our veracity score (0 = FALSE, 100 = TRUE) **
100/100

** Our explanation for veracity score **
The claim is accurate. The UK has implemented several initiatives to protect critical infrastructure sectors from cyber threats. In 2018, the UK government introduced the Network and Information Systems (NIS) Regulations to enhance the security of essential services, including energy, healthcare, and finance. These regulations require operators to implement robust safeguards and report significant incidents. Additionally, the National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) provides guidance and support to these sectors to bolster their cyber resilience. More recently, in 2024, the UK designated data centres as critical national infrastructure, granting them greater protection against cyber-attacks and IT disruptions. These measures collectively demonstrate the UK's commitment to safeguarding its critical infrastructure from cyber threats. Sources: [UK Government News, 2018](https://www.gov.uk/government/news/tough-new-rules-to-protect-uks-critical-infrastructure-come-into-force), [The Guardian, 2024](https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2024/sep/12/uk-datacentres-critical-infrastructure-cyber-protection).
