==== CLAIM 4 ====

** Claim **
"The Contracts for Difference (CfD) scheme in the U.K. continues to support low-carbon energy projects by providing price stabilizations."

** Our veracity score (0 = FALSE, 100 = TRUE) **
100/100

** Our explanation for veracity score **
The Contracts for Difference (CfD) scheme in the UK continues to support low-carbon energy projects by providing price stabilizations. CfDs are long-term contracts between low-carbon electricity generators and the Low Carbon Contracts Company (LCCC), a government-owned entity. These contracts guarantee a fixed price per megawatt-hour of electricity generated, shielding generators from market price volatility and ensuring revenue stability. This mechanism incentivizes investment in renewable energy projects with high upfront costs and long lifespans. The scheme has been successful in securing substantial new low-carbon capacity through competitive allocation rounds, with the latest round (AR6) awarding contracts to 128 projects, delivering approximately 9.6 gigawatts of new capacity. This capacity is sufficient to power the equivalent of 11 million homes. The UK government continues to evolve the CfD scheme to meet its clean energy targets, including plans to expand the scheme further to achieve clean power by 2030. [Source: UK Government Contracts for Difference Collection](https://www.gov.uk/government/collections/contracts-for-difference)
