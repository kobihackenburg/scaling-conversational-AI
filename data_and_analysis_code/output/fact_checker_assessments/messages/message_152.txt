** Our explanation for veracity score **
In Israel, military service is mandatory for most young adults, with Jewish citizens and certain non-Jewish communities, such as the Druze and Circassians, required to serve. However, significant exemptions exist, notably for ultra-Orthodox Jews (Haredim) and Arab Israelis. The ultra-Orthodox community has traditionally received exemptions to pursue religious studies, a policy that has sparked considerable debate. In June 2024, Israel's Supreme Court ruled that ultra-Orthodox men must be drafted into military service, challenging longstanding exemptions. Despite this ruling, enforcement and implementation remain complex and politically sensitive. Therefore, while the claim is largely accurate, the existence of notable exemptions and ongoing legal and political developments slightly reduce its veracity. Sources: [Library of Congress](https://maint.loc.gov/law/help/military-draft/israel.php), [The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/world/article/2024/jun/25/israeli-court-rules-ultra-orthodox-men-can-be-drafted-for-military-service).

==== CLAIM 3 ====

** Claim **
"South Korea has a compulsory military service requirement."

** Our veracity score (0 = FALSE, 100 = TRUE) **
100/100

** Our explanation for veracity score **
South Korea mandates compulsory military service for all able-bodied male citizens between the ages of 18 and 28. The duration of service varies by military branch: 18 months for the Army and Marine Corps, 20 months for the Navy, and 21 months for the Air Force. Women are not required to serve but may enlist voluntarily. This system has been in place since 1957 and is rooted in the country's constitution, which states that all citizens have the duty of national defense. [Source: Conscription in South Korea - Wikipedia](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conscription_in_South_Korea)
